**Tone:** Premium, technically authoritative, B2B-focused. Not salesy or consumer-grade.

#### Section 6: FAQ Block (5 Questions)

**Q1: What is pixel pitch and why does it matter?**
Pixel pitch measures the distance in millimeters between the center of one LED pixel and the center of the adjacent pixel. A smaller number means higher pixel density and sharper images at closer viewing distances. For a {pitch}mm display, individual pixels become imperceptible at approximately {min distance} feet.

**Q2: Is {pitch}mm better than {alternative pitch}mm?**
Neither is inherently better — the right pitch depends on your viewing distance. {Pitch}mm provides higher resolution per square foot than {alternative}mm, making it superior for close-viewing applications. However, if your nearest viewer is {distance}+ feet away, {alternative}mm delivers comparable perceived quality at a lower cost per square foot.

**Q3: Can I mix {pitch}mm panels with other pixel pitches?**
Panels of different pixel pitches cannot be seamlessly combined in a single display surface. However, BrightLink can design multi-display environments where different walls or zones use different pitches optimized for their specific viewing conditions.

**Q4: What content resolution do I need for a {pitch}mm wall?**
For best results, feed your {pitch}mm wall native-resolution content matching the panel's total pixel count. A {example size}" configuration at {pitch}mm has a native resolution of {res}, so 4K source content will display at full fidelity. Lower-resolution content will be upscaled, which can reduce perceived sharpness.

**Q5: How does {pitch}mm compare to an LCD video wall?**
At {pitch}mm, a direct view LED wall eliminates the bezels that segment LCD video walls into visible grid lines. LED also delivers higher brightness (typically {nits}+ nits vs. 500-700 nits for LCD), wider viewing angles, and longer operational life with no backlight degradation. The total cost of ownership over 7-10 years is comparable to or lower than LCD.

#### Section 5: FAQ

**Q1: Is LED always more expensive than LCD?**
LED has a higher upfront cost per square foot, but the total cost of ownership over 7-10 years is often comparable or lower. LED walls require no backlight replacement, consume less energy at equivalent brightness, and individual modules can be swapped without replacing the entire display.

**Q2: Can I replace my existing LCD video wall with LED?**
Yes. LED walls can be mounted using similar structural supports. The transition typically requires updating the mounting framework and video controller, but existing content systems and source equipment usually remain compatible.

**Q3: Which looks better — LCD or LED?**
At equivalent resolution, direct view LED produces a superior image due to zero bezels, higher native contrast, and wider viewing angles. The visual difference is most noticeable with content that spans the full display — video, presentations, and digital art appear as a single continuous image on LED, versus a grid of framed panels on LCD.

### Answer Structure Guidelines

1. **Lead with the direct answer** — Don't bury the answer under context. Google pulls featured snippets from the first substantive paragraph.
2. **Use specific numbers where possible** — "100,000+ hours" is stronger than "a very long time." "1,000-2,000 nits" is stronger than "very bright."
3. **Include a comparison or benchmark** — Readers searching for answers want to evaluate. Give them something to compare against.
4. **End with a soft CTA** — Don't hard-sell in FAQ content. Instead: "Contact our team for specific pricing on your configuration."
5. **Optimal answer length:** 50-80 words for the direct answer (snippet target), 200-400 words for the full article.

**5. Comparison Page (LCD vs LED):**
If you're evaluating LCD and LED for a commercial video wall, the decision comes down to three factors: how long the installation needs to last, how much ambient light the space gets, and whether visible bezels between panels are acceptable. LCD video walls cost less upfront but introduce seam lines between every panel, max out at 500-700 nits of brightness, and require backlight replacement every 60,000-80,000 hours. Direct view LED eliminates bezels entirely, delivers 1,000-2,000+ nits, and operates for 100,000+ hours — making it the preferred technology for permanent commercial installations where image quality and reliability drive the decision.
